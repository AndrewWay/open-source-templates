Open Source Templates

The Curious Case of the Issue and Pull Request Template

by Tal Ater

Chapter 1

The Overflowing Inbox

You are standing in an open field west of a white house. The house is a carefully constructed masterpiece, with solid foundations and a beautiful facade matched by an elegant interior.

In fact, the house is such a masterpiece that an endless line of volunteers has taken the time to help you improve upon it.

There are two mailboxes here.

The first mailbox is simply labeled issues ("as if I don't have enough of those to deal with", you think to yourself).

The second is mysteriously labeled Pull Requests.

Both are overflowing.

  • To organize the issues box, turn to page 37.
  • To organize the pull requests box, turn to page 75.
Page 37

Bottomless

You take a deep breath and open the box labeled issues.

The size of the box surprises you. As you look into it, you are suddenly gripped by a feeling that the box has no bottom.

As you stare down into the seemingly bottomless pit of the box, you begin to feel a chill running down your spine. It is as if… as if the endless box of issues is staring right back into your soul.

It feels judgy.

  • Rolling up your sleeves, you decide to take charge of the issues box and turn to page 38.
  • Fear overtakes you. You close the box with a high pitched yelp and turn back to page 1.
Page 38

Angels and Demons

As you reach down into the box, a strange order begins to form. You start to notice patterns in the issues.

Some take the form of bug reports, grey festering blobs of infinite malevolence. Others manifest themselves as great ethereal balls of living energy in the form ideas for features, improvements and changes.

You furrow your brow as you consider concentrating on one type of issue, or both.

  • You decide to focus on bug reports.
  • You decide to priorize new features, improvements and changes.
  • You decide to accept both bug reports and proposals for new features, improvements and changes.
Page 39

Two Sides to Every Schwartz

As you pull your head out of the issues mailbox, you are startled by a voice coming from behind you.

"So… what are you?"

They are standing under a tree, each with an arm round the other’s neck, and you know which is which in a moment, because one of them has `front’ embroidered on his collar, and the other `back.’

"I'm sure I'm very sorry, but whatever do you mean?"

"There are two sides to every Schwartz," says the first. "Front end…"

"…and back end," adds his brother.

  • "My project runs in the browser" you say, giving back a mistrusting look.
  • "I am all about the server" you say with some pride.
  • You roll your eyes at the twins and say "How quaint. Actually my code runs equally well on the server, as well as the browser".
Page 42

An Unexpected Guest

The brothers nod with understanding, give each other a knowing look and dissappear into thin air.

"Ohh well," sighs a voice from the tree above, startling you.

You look up just in time to see The Creature's grin widen at your fright. You notice with alarm that it has very long claws and a great many teeth.

"Would you tell me, please, which way I ought to go from here?" you ask.

"That depends a good deal on where you want to get to," says The Creature.
